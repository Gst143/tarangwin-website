Find answers to common questions about Tarang Win

Getting Started

Download the Tarang Win app from Google Play Store. Open the app, enter your mobile number, and verify it using the OTP sent to your phone. Complete your profile by entering your name and you're ready to start!

Yes! Downloading and registering on Tarang Win is completely free. We also offer free practice contests. However, paid contests require an entry fee, which you can add to your wallet using various payment methods.

Tarang Win requires Android 6.0 or higher. The app is optimized for most Android smartphones and tablets. Ensure you have a stable internet connection for the best experience.

Contests & Gameplay

Browse available contests, pay the entry fee, and join before the contest starts. When the contest begins, answer multiple-choice questions within the time limit. Your score and completion time determine your ranking on the leaderboard.

Winners are selected based on a skill-based ranking system: 1) Highest score, 2) Fastest completion time, 3) Earliest submission time. This ensures fair competition based purely on skill.

We offer four types of contests: Daily (small entry fees, quick quizzes), Mega (high stakes, large prizes), Practice (free, no risk), and Premium (exclusive, expert-level questions with massive rewards).

Yes! You can join as many contests as you want, as long as you have sufficient wallet balance and the contest timings don't overlap.

Wallet & Payments

Go to the Wallet section, tap "Add Money", enter the amount, and choose your payment method (UPI, Net Banking, Debit Card, or Credit Card). Complete the payment securely via Cashfree gateway.

The minimum withdrawal amount is ₹100. You can withdraw your winnings to your UPI ID after admin approval.

Withdrawal requests are typically processed within 24 hours after admin approval. The time taken for the amount to reflect in your bank account depends on your bank's processing time.

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is deducted from prizes above ₹10,000 at a rate of 30%. For example, if you win ₹15,000, ₹4,500 (30% TDS) will be deducted, and you'll receive ₹10,500.
